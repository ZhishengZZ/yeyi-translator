This deliberately long paragraph exists so the extension can exercise sentence-level splitting for very long text units. When a single paragraph exceeds the per-item character budget, the background worker breaks it into ordered sentence chunks and translates each chunk separately. If one of those chunks fails or is dropped by the model, the remaining chunks must still be assembled and shown instead of blanking the entire paragraph. This behavior matters most on the free rate-limited tier, where a single dropped chunk used to erase a whole section of translated text. Readers expect to see as much of the translation as the model actually returned, not an empty gap where a long passage should be. A robust translator therefore stitches together whatever parts came back, keeps their original order, and only marks the paragraph as failed when absolutely nothing was returned for it. This paragraph is padded with several additional sentences so that its total length comfortably exceeds the twelve hundred character threshold used by the smoke test configuration, guaranteeing that the splitting path is taken and that at least two ordered parts are produced for assembly. Even with one part intentionally missing, the visible translation should remain non-empty and readable.
